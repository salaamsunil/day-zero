<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-circle-info"></i> Why Dietary Documentation Matters</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Under the <strong>Aged Care Quality Standards</strong> (particularly Standards 1, 3, and 5), providers must demonstrate that residents' nutritional, dietary, and cultural food preferences are identified, recorded, and consistently acted upon. Inspectors look for evidence in both the clinical record and day-to-day operational records.</p>
<p class="section-intro">Poor dietary documentation is one of the most common causes of non-compliance findings. This checklist covers the six core documentation categories that inspectors examine. Use it to audit your current practices and identify gaps before an ACQSC assessment.</p>
<div class="risk-box risk-low">
<h4><i class="fas fa-check-circle" style="color:#22c55e"></i> &nbsp; Standard 1: Consumer Dignity and Choice</h4>
<p>Residents' food preferences, cultural and religious requirements, and dislikes must be documented and reflected in menu planning and meal delivery. Evidence of choice and self-determination is assessed.</p>
</div>
<div class="risk-box risk-medium" style="margin-top:0.75rem">
<h4><i class="fas fa-stethoscope" style="color:#d97706"></i> &nbsp; Standard 3: Personal Care and Clinical Care</h4>
<p>Nutritional needs must be assessed, risks (including malnutrition) identified, and outcomes monitored. Includes MUST screening, weight records, dietitian referrals, IDDSI compliance, and allergen management.</p>
</div>
<div class="risk-box risk-high" style="margin-top:0.75rem">
<h4><i class="fas fa-utensils" style="color:#ef4444"></i> &nbsp; Standard 5: Organisation's Service Environment</h4>
<p>The environment in which food is prepared and delivered must be safe. This includes documented allergy management processes, kitchen IDDSI preparation protocols, and staff training records.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-id-card"></i> 1. Resident Dietary Profile</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Every resident must have a current dietary profile on admission and updated whenever their needs change. This is the foundation document from which all meal planning, ordering, and allergen management flows.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Solid food texture requirement (IDDSI level)</div><div class="item-note">Must use the correct IDDSI terminology (e.g., Level 5 Minced &amp; Moist, Level 6 Soft &amp; Bite-Sized), not informal descriptors like "soft" or "chopped"</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Fluid texture requirement (IDDSI level)</div><div class="item-note">e.g., Level 2 Mildly Thick, Level 3 Moderately Thick, must be assessed by a Speech Pathologist for levels 1–4</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Documented allergens with clinical source</div><div class="item-note">Distinguish medically confirmed allergies from intolerances and preferences; source noted (self-reported, medical record, GP letter)</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Dietary restrictions (medical/clinical)</div><div class="item-note">e.g., diabetic diet, renal diet, low-FODMAP, must reference the clinical basis (GP or dietitian recommendation)</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Food preferences, cultural and religious requirements</div><div class="item-note">e.g., Halal, Kosher, vegan, country of origin preferences, documented as resident-reported</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Food dislikes</div><div class="item-note">Often overlooked, should be documented to prevent repeated meal rejections that can affect intake</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Oral health and dentition notes relevant to texture</div><div class="item-note">e.g., full dentures, missing posterior teeth, recent extraction, informs texture level choice</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Profile review date and responsible staff member</div><div class="item-note">Must be reviewed at minimum quarterly, or after any clinical event affecting eating/swallowing</div></div></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-shield-halved"></i> 2. Allergen Management Documentation</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Allergen errors in aged care can be fatal. Documentation must be comprehensive, accessible at point of care, and built into the ordering and meal delivery workflow.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Allergen register: all residents with confirmed allergies listed</div><div class="item-note">Should be accessible by kitchen staff at all times, not just in clinical notes</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Allergen information recorded against each menu item</div><div class="item-note">Kitchen must know which dishes contain which allergens before service, not after</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Conflict checks performed before orders are placed</div><div class="item-note">Process for identifying when a menu item conflicts with a resident's allergen profile, ideally at ordering stage</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Tray labels or delivery cards indicating allergen requirements</div><div class="item-note">Visible allergen alerts at point of delivery to reduce risk of wrong meal being served</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Staff training records for allergen awareness</div><div class="item-note">Both kitchen and care staff must be trained; records must show completion dates and staff names</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Incident report for any allergen exposure event</div><div class="item-note">Even near-misses must be documented, investigated, and actioned</div></div></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-clipboard-list"></i> 3. Meal Ordering &amp; Delivery Records</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Facilities must be able to demonstrate that meals ordered and delivered match the resident's care plan requirements. This is frequently checked against intake records during audits.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Daily meal orders recorded per resident per meal period</div><div class="item-note">Records must show what was ordered, not just that a meal was served, to allow retrospective dietary review</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Delivery confirmation recorded (confirmed/delivered status)</div><div class="item-note">Separate from ordering, confirms the resident received the meal, not just that it was prepared</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Food intake percentage recorded at each meal</div><div class="item-note">Required for MUST medium/high risk residents; best practice for all; supports early identification of declining intake</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Meal refusals documented with reason</div><div class="item-note">Repeated refusals must be escalated to clinical lead and noted in care plan, not just left as a blank record</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Waste notes for low-intake meals</div><div class="item-note">Descriptive note of what was left and any resident comments, valuable clinical context beyond a percentage figure</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Supplementary nutrition intake recorded (oral supplements, fortified foods)</div><div class="item-note">If supplements are prescribed, their intake must also be recorded, often an audit gap</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Between-meal snacks and fluid intake records where clinically indicated</div><div class="item-note">Relevant for dehydration risk, fluid-thickening compliance, and MUST medium/high residents</div></div></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-notes-medical"></i> 4. Care Plan: Nutrition &amp; Dietary Section</h2>
<p class="section-intro">The care plan nutrition section must translate the resident's dietary profile into specific, measurable goals and actions. Inspectors look for evidence that the care plan is current, individualised, and reflected in practice.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Documented nutritional goals (calorie and protein targets)</div><div class="item-note">Minimum 30 kcal/kg/day and 1.0–1.35 g protein/kg/day (ESPEN 2022); goals must be individualised, not generic</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">IDDSI texture level specified and rationale documented</div><div class="item-note">Care plan must state the level (not just "soft diet") and reference the assessment that determined it (e.g., SLP assessment dated DD/MM/YYYY)</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Current MUST score and risk category recorded</div><div class="item-note">Must be updated after each screening, not just at admission</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Dietitian involvement documented where applicable</div><div class="item-note">Date of referral, date seen, and any dietitian recommendations must appear in both the dietitian note and the care plan</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Actions for poor intake or weight loss specified</div><div class="item-note">e.g., "If intake drops below 50% at two consecutive meals, notify care coordinator", must be a specific escalation pathway</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Care plan review date and responsible clinician</div><div class="item-note">Minimum quarterly review; after hospitalisation; after any significant change in condition</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Resident (or representative) signature confirming goals discussed</div><div class="item-note">Demonstrates informed consent and person-centred care, required under Standard 1</div></div></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-weight-scale"></i> 5. Weight &amp; Clinical Monitoring Records</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Weight is a primary indicator of nutritional status in aged care. All weight records must be attributable, dated, and linked to a clinical response when thresholds are crossed.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Monthly weight recorded on calibrated scales</div><div class="item-note">Paper-based records must note scale ID and calibration date; digital systems should record timestamp and staff name automatically</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Weight trend tracked over time (minimum 3 months visible)</div><div class="item-note">A single weight reading is insufficient, inspectors want to see trend data to assess trajectory</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Unplanned weight loss of ≥ 3 kg or ≥ 5% flagged and actioned</div><div class="item-note">Documentation must show the flagging, who was notified, what was done, and the timeline, a "clinical concern" note alone is insufficient</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">3-day food and fluid diary completed for medium/high MUST risk</div><div class="item-note">Must cover three consecutive days including a weekend day; completed by care staff at point of care</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Incident reports for clinical nutrition events (choking, aspiration, severe refusal)</div><div class="item-note">Every incident must be reported, investigated, and linked back to care plan update</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">MUST screening repeated after hospitalisation or acute illness</div><div class="item-note">Residents returning from hospital commonly have changed nutritional status, a new MUST score must be completed within 48 hours of return</div></div></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-utensils"></i> 6. Kitchen &amp; Menu Documentation</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Kitchen documentation ensures that meal planning is nutritionally adequate, IDDSI-compliant, and consistent with residents' dietary profiles. This is often the weakest documentation area in many facilities.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Weekly or fortnightly menu in advance and accessible to residents</div><div class="item-note">Residents must be able to make informed choices, menus should be available at least one day before the meal period</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Menu reviewed by dietitian (minimum annually, or when significantly changed)</div><div class="item-note">Dietitian sign-off date must be documented on the menu or in the governance record</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Allergen content of each menu item documented in kitchen records</div><div class="item-note">Kitchen must have ingredient-level allergen information, labelling on tins/packets is not sufficient for multi-component dishes</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">IDDSI texture preparation instructions for each menu item at each texture level</div><div class="item-note">Must describe the preparation method, not just the target level, e.g., "blend X with Y until smooth, pass through sieve" for Level 4 Pureed</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Portion sizes and caloric estimates documented</div><div class="item-note">Supports nutritional compliance reporting and dietitian review; essential for ESPEN 2022 calorie target verification</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Substitute options documented for each meal period</div><div class="item-note">What alternative is offered when a resident refuses the menu item or it conflicts with their dietary profile?</div></div></li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i><div><div class="item-label">Kitchen prep checklist completed and signed daily</div><div class="item-note">Confirms IDDSI textures were prepared correctly before service; must be retained for audit</div></div></li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation" style="color:#EAB308"></i> Most Common Audit Gaps</h2>
<p class="section-intro">These are the dietary documentation failures most frequently cited in ACQSC non-compliance findings.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Dietary profiles not updated after hospitalisation or clinical change, admission profile treated as permanent</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>IDDSI levels stated in informal terms ("soft", "minced") rather than the standardised IDDSI framework level names</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Allergen records in clinical notes only, kitchen staff cannot access them at point of food preparation</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Meal delivery records showing orders placed but no intake or delivery confirmation</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Care plan nutrition goals stated generically ("maintain weight") rather than with specific, measurable targets</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Weight loss flagged in a progress note but no care plan update or dietitian referral documented as a result</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Menu reviewed by dietitian but no sign-off date or dietitian name recorded on the menu document</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Fluid-thickening orders not matched against kitchen prep records, thickened fluids prepared inconsistently</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>No escalation pathway documented, staff know to flag poor intake but there is no written procedure specifying who, when, and how</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Cultural or religious dietary requirements noted at admission but not reflected in the weekly menu or meal order system</li>
</ul>
</div>
