<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-circle-info"></i> What is MUST?</h2>
<p class="section-intro">The Malnutrition Universal Screening Tool (MUST) is a five-item screening tool developed by the British Association for Parenteral and Enteral Nutrition (BAPEN). It is widely adopted in Australian aged care to identify adults who are malnourished or at risk of malnutrition, and to guide appropriate clinical action.</p>
<p class="section-intro" style="margin-bottom:0">MUST is not a diagnostic tool, it is a <strong>screening</strong> tool. A positive screen (score ≥ 1) should prompt further assessment, not a definitive diagnosis. All aged care facilities operating under the Aged Care Quality Standards are expected to have a malnutrition screening process in place and to demonstrate action taken when residents screen positive.</p>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-list-ol"></i> The 3-Step MUST Scoring Process</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Add the scores from all three steps to calculate the overall MUST score.</p>
<table class="iddsi-table">
<thead>
<tr><th>Step</th><th>What to Measure</th><th>Score</th></tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><strong>Step 1: BMI</strong></td><td>BMI &gt; 20: <strong>0 pts</strong><br>BMI 18.5–20: <strong>1 pt</strong><br>BMI &lt; 18.5: <strong>2 pts</strong></td><td>0, 1, or 2</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Step 2: Weight Loss</strong></td><td>Unplanned loss &lt; 5%: <strong>0 pts</strong><br>Unplanned loss 5–10%: <strong>1 pt</strong><br>Unplanned loss &gt; 10%: <strong>2 pts</strong></td><td>0, 1, or 2</td></tr>
<tr><td><strong>Step 3: Acute Disease Effect</strong></td><td>If acutely ill and no nutritional intake likely for &gt; 5 days: <strong>2 pts</strong><br>Otherwise: <strong>0 pts</strong></td><td>0 or 2</td></tr>
<tr style="background:#f9fafb; font-weight:700;"><td>Total MUST Score</td><td>Add Step 1 + Step 2 + Step 3</td><td>0 – 6</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>
<p style="font-size:0.88rem; color:var(--gray); margin-top:0.75rem;"><strong>BMI estimation:</strong> If height/weight cannot be measured directly, use mid-upper arm circumference (MUAC) or ulna length to estimate. Document the method used.</p>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-chart-bar"></i> Risk Categories &amp; Required Actions</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Each MUST score maps to a risk category with specific clinical actions required.</p>
<div class="risk-box risk-low">
<h4><span class="score-badge score-low">Score 0: Low Risk</span> &nbsp; Routine Care</h4>
<p>Continue routine clinical care. Repeat MUST screening as per facility policy, typically monthly for all residents. No immediate dietary intervention required, but monitor for any change in condition that may increase risk.</p>
</div>
<div class="risk-box risk-medium">
<h4><span class="score-badge score-medium">Score 1: Medium Risk</span> &nbsp; Observe &amp; Monitor</h4>
<p>Document 3-day dietary intake to assess actual consumption. Repeat MUST screening weekly. If intake is adequate, continue monitoring. If intake is poor, consider dietary fortification or supplementation and refer to dietitian.</p>
</div>
<div class="risk-box risk-high">
<h4><span class="score-badge score-high">Score 2+: High Risk</span> &nbsp; Treat</h4>
<p>Refer to dietitian within 24–48 hours. Set specific nutritional goals in the care plan (calorie and protein targets). Monitor weight and intake weekly. Review at each care plan meeting. Document all actions taken. Consider nutritional supplements if dietary intake alone is insufficient.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-clipboard-check"></i> Documentation Requirements (Aged Care Quality Standards)</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Under the Aged Care Quality Standards (Standard 3: Personal Care and Clinical Care), facilities must demonstrate that residents' nutritional needs are identified, assessed, and met. The following documentation is expected during an ACQSC audit.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Initial MUST score recorded on admission (within 24 hours)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>MUST screening repeated at minimum monthly for all residents</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Weight recorded at each MUST assessment using calibrated scales</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Height or estimated height (ulna length / knee height method) documented</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Unplanned weight loss of ≥ 5% flagged to clinical lead within 24 hours</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Dietitian referral documented when MUST score reaches 2 or above</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Care plan updated to reflect nutritional goals, actions, and review dates</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>3-day dietary intake record completed for all medium-risk residents</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Nutritional supplement prescription and intake recorded where applicable</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>MUST outcomes reviewed at monthly clinical governance meetings</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>All scores and actions recorded in the resident's clinical record with timestamp and staff name</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation" style="color:#EAB308"></i> Common Pitfalls to Avoid</h2>
<p class="section-intro">These are the most common errors found during ACQSC audits related to malnutrition screening.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Using estimated weight rather than measured weight, always weigh residents monthly on calibrated scales</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Forgetting Step 3 (acute disease effect), commonly omitted, especially when residents are hospitalised</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Failing to update the care plan when MUST score changes risk category</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>No dietitian referral documented when score reaches 2+, this is a clinical requirement, not optional</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Screening only on admission and not repeating monthly, ongoing monitoring is mandatory</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>Recording MUST scores in paper files that are difficult to retrieve during an audit</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-triangle-exclamation"></i>No escalation pathway defined, staff must know who to notify and when for high-risk scores</li>
</ul>
</div>
