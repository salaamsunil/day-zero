<h2><i class="fas fa-layer-group"></i> IDDSI Framework: Quick Reference</h2>
<p class="section-intro">The International Dysphagia Diet Standardisation Initiative (IDDSI) provides a globally standardised framework for describing food textures and drink thicknesses. Australian aged care facilities must align to these standards under the Aged Care Quality Standards.</p>
<table class="iddsi-table">
<thead>
<tr><th>Level</th><th>Name</th><th>Type</th><th>Key Characteristics</th></tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-0">0</span></td><td><strong>Thin</strong></td><td>Drink</td><td>Water, juice, milk. Flows like water.</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-1">1</span></td><td><strong>Slightly Thick</strong></td><td>Drink</td><td>Thicker than water, flows through a teat</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-2">2</span></td><td><strong>Mildly Thick</strong></td><td>Drink</td><td>Pours off a spoon, effort needed to drink from cup</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-3">3</span></td><td><strong>Liquidised</strong></td><td>Drink / Food</td><td>Smooth, no lumps, can be drunk from cup or syringe</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-4">4</span></td><td><strong>Pureed</strong></td><td>Food</td><td>Smooth cohesive, no lumps, cannot be piped or molded</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-5">5</span></td><td><strong>Minced &amp; Moist</strong></td><td>Food</td><td>Small particles (4mm), moist, no separate thin liquid</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-6">6</span></td><td><strong>Soft &amp; Bite-Sized</strong></td><td>Food</td><td>Tender, can be mashed with tongue, max 15mm pieces</td></tr>
<tr><td><span class="iddsi-level level-7">7</span></td><td><strong>Regular / Easy to Chew</strong></td><td>Food</td><td>Normal diet with consideration for chewing ability</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-user-check"></i> 1. Resident Dietary Profile Documentation</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Each resident must have a current, signed dietary profile on file. Review and update at least every 3 months, or when condition changes.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Resident IDDSI texture level assigned and documented (Levels 0–7)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Texture prescription signed by a Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP) or dietitian</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>All allergens and intolerances documented (including cross-contamination risks)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Cultural, religious, and personal food preferences recorded</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Thickener type and ratio specified for drink modifications</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Assistance requirements noted (e.g., staff to sit beside, adaptive utensils)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Profile reviewed and re-signed after acute illness or hospitalization</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Next review date scheduled and tracked</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Resident and/or family informed of and consent to current texture level</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Profile accessible to kitchen and care staff at point of meal service</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-utensils"></i> 2. Meal Ordering &amp; Planning</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Meal ordering systems must cross-check resident profiles at point of order to prevent incorrect texture meals reaching residents.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Meal ordering process checks resident IDDSI level before confirming order</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Allergen conflict check performed at order time (not just profile entry)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Meals for each texture level clearly differentiated on kitchen production sheets</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Menu options available at each IDDSI level for all meal periods</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Fortified or modified meals labeled separately from standard meals</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Meal counts per texture level communicated to kitchen before each service</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Process for last-minute dietary changes communicated to kitchen</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Guest/visitor meals handled without cross-contaminating resident-specific production</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-fire-burner"></i> 3. Kitchen Preparation &amp; Verification</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Kitchen staff must be able to verify their output meets IDDSI standards before meals leave the kitchen. Visual spot-checks are insufficient; use standardised testing methods.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Fork drip test used for Levels 1–4 drinks and foods</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Spoon tilt test used for purees and thickened drinks</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Finger test (pressing food) used for Levels 5–7</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Particle size checked against IDDSI specifications (e.g. 4mm for Level 5)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Thickener preparation follows manufacturer-specified ratios by weight, not by eye</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Kitchen production records show texture level for each batch prepared</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Separate equipment/utensils for allergen-modified meals</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Temperature checks documented before meal delivery (hot &gt;60°C, cold &lt;5°C)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Plating standards for each texture level are documented with photos</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Batch labels on modified meals include texture level and allergen flags</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-graduation-cap"></i> 4. Staff Training &amp; Competency</h2>
<p class="section-intro">All staff involved in meal ordering, preparation, or service must demonstrate competency in IDDSI. Training records must be current and accessible to auditors.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>All kitchen and care staff completed IDDSI induction training (document date)</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Annual IDDSI competency refresher completed for all relevant staff</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Thickener preparation training completed and logged per staff member</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Training records include staff name, date, trainer, and competency outcome</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>New staff complete IDDSI training before unsupervised meal service</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Agency/casual staff briefed on facility-specific IDDSI requirements before shift</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Escalation pathway known: who to call if unsure about a resident's texture level</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>SLP contact details available to kitchen and care staff</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="checklist-section">
<h2><i class="fas fa-clipboard-check"></i> 5. Audit &amp; Continuous Improvement</h2>
<p class="section-intro">Regular self-auditing against IDDSI standards is evidence of a well-managed quality system and will be reviewed by the Aged Care Quality and Safety Commission.</p>
<ul class="checklist">
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Monthly internal dietary audit conducted and documented</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Mealtime observation audit: staff observe residents during meals quarterly</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Incident register captures any texture-related adverse events</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Resident and family feedback on meals collected and acted on</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Corrective actions from previous audits are documented and closed out</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>IDDSI policy and procedures reviewed annually</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Dietitian involvement in menu review at least annually</li>
<li><i class="fas fa-square-check"></i>Emergency/evacuation plan accounts for residents requiring modified textures</li>
</ul>
</div>
