Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 May 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Day Zero Solutions Pty Ltd (ACN 698 240 907, ABN 95 698 240 907) ("Day Zero", "we", "us", "our") collects, uses, holds, and discloses personal information in connection with our website at day-zero.com.au and our products: NutriCare, RestroAI, and QueueZero.

We comply with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). This policy is written in plain English and applies to all personal information we handle.

1. Scope and what this policy covers

This policy applies to:

  • Our website at day-zero.com.au (including resource pages and lead-magnet forms).
  • NutriCare: a dietary management platform for senior care and aged care facilities, accessed at nutri-care.app.
  • RestroAI: a restaurant management platform for inventory, procurement, recipes, staff, ordering, and analytics.
  • QueueZero: a mobile ordering platform for food trucks and pop-up vendors (in development).

If any of our products or product domains have a separate, more specific privacy notice, that notice applies in addition to this policy. Where there is a conflict, the product-specific notice prevails for that product.

2. The personal information we collect

We collect different categories of personal information depending on how you interact with us.

2.1 Website visitors and prospects

  • Contact details you provide via the contact form, demo booking form, chat widget, waitlist signup, or lead-magnet downloads: name, email address, company or facility name, role, and message content.
  • Product interest: which product you are enquiring about.
  • Usage data: pages viewed, session duration, approximate location (from IP), device type, browser, and referral source, collected automatically via analytics.

2.2 Product users (people who log in to our products)

When you, or a person at one of our customer organisations, creates or uses an account in NutriCare, RestroAI, or QueueZero, we collect:

  • Account details: name, email address, password (hashed and salted), role and permissions, organisation or facility you belong to, and (where used) profile photo.
  • Authentication data: login timestamps, session identifiers, and security signals.
  • Usage data: actions taken in the product, pages and features used, configuration and preferences, audit logs.
  • Support communications: messages you send us through email, chat, or in-product support.

For some workforce features in RestroAI (clock-in kiosk), staff at customer venues may submit a PIN and a clock-in photo. That data belongs to and is controlled by the customer organisation; we process it on their behalf.

2.3 Information we process on behalf of our customers ("Customer Data")

Our products are used by businesses (aged care facilities, restaurants, food trucks). Those businesses are our customers, and they enter information into our products to run their operations. That information may include personal information about their residents, employees, customers, and suppliers. We refer to this as "Customer Data".

For Customer Data, our customer is the data controller and Day Zero is the data processor. We process Customer Data on our customer's behalf, under our agreement with them, and only for the purposes of providing the product. Examples by product:

ProductExamples of Customer Data we process
NutriCare Resident profiles (name, date of birth, room, photograph), dietary requirements, allergens, IDDSI texture levels, food preferences and dislikes, medical conditions relevant to diet, meal-order history, and clinical notes entered by facility staff. This may include health information, which is sensitive information under the Privacy Act.
RestroAI Employee records (name, role, contact, roster, clock-in times, PIN, payslip data), supplier contact details, customer ordering details (name, contact, order history, dietary notes if entered), reservation details, loyalty profile data.
QueueZero Vendor (food truck) account and business details; end-customer order details where the customer chooses to provide a name, contact, or location for pickup; and order history.

If you are an individual whose information is in one of our products because a customer organisation entered it (for example, you are a resident of an aged care facility, or an employee of a restaurant that uses RestroAI), please direct any privacy enquiries to that organisation in the first instance. We will assist them in responding to you.

2.4 Payment information

We use Stripe to process subscription payments from our customers. Stripe collects and processes card details directly; we do not see or store card numbers. We receive limited billing information from Stripe (name on card, last four digits, country, billing email, invoice and subscription status) to manage subscriptions and reconcile invoices. Stripe's privacy policy is at stripe.com/au/privacy.

2.5 Sensitive information

We treat health information processed in NutriCare (such as allergens, IDDSI texture levels, and dietary medical conditions) as sensitive information. We only collect and process it on behalf of facilities, with the consent and authority that the facility has obtained from residents or their representatives, and only for the purpose of providing the NutriCare service. Facilities are responsible for obtaining the consents required under the Privacy Act and relevant aged care laws.

3. How we use personal information

We use personal information to:

  • Respond to enquiries, provide demos, and follow up on lead-magnet downloads.
  • Create, secure, and operate user accounts in our products.
  • Deliver, maintain, support, and improve our products and website.
  • Process subscription payments and manage billing.
  • Send service messages (security alerts, billing notices, product updates that affect your use of the service).
  • Send product news and educational content where you have opted in. You can unsubscribe at any time.
  • Detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, security incidents, and violations of our Terms of Service.
  • Comply with our legal obligations, including responses to lawful requests by public authorities.
  • Produce aggregated and de-identified analytics to understand product usage and improve the service. De-identified data is not used to re-identify individuals.

4. Disclosure of personal information

We do not sell personal information. We share it only in the situations below.

4.2 Other disclosures

  • Within our customer organisations: where you are a user of a product, information about your use may be visible to authorised staff of the customer organisation that owns the account (for example, an administrator of an aged care facility can see staff activity within that facility's tenant).
  • Professional advisors: lawyers, accountants, insurers, and auditors, under confidentiality.
  • Legal and safety: where required by law, regulator request, court order, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of Day Zero, our users, or the public.
  • Business transfers: in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to confidentiality and continuity of this policy.

5. Cross-border data transfers

Some of our service providers are located outside Australia (see the table above). When we transfer personal information overseas, we take reasonable steps to ensure the recipient handles the information in a way consistent with the APPs, including by using providers that publish strong security and privacy commitments and by entering into appropriate contracts where required.

6. How we store and protect information

We take security seriously. Our practices include:

  • Encryption in transit (TLS/HTTPS) and at rest for product databases and backups.
  • Role-based access control and row-level security in our products, so customer organisations only see their own data.
  • Strong authentication, with multi-factor authentication available for product accounts.
  • Logging, monitoring, and audit trails for sensitive operations.
  • Vetted hosting providers (Supabase, Vercel) with their own security certifications.
  • Internal access on a need-to-know basis, under confidentiality.

No system can guarantee absolute security. If you have reason to believe that an interaction with us is no longer secure, please contact us immediately.

7. How long we keep information

We keep personal information for as long as needed for the purposes set out in this policy and to meet legal, accounting, and reporting obligations.

  • Website enquiries and waitlists: kept while we are still in dialogue with you, then archived or deleted on request.
  • Product accounts and Customer Data: kept for the duration of the customer's subscription. On termination, we delete or return Customer Data within a reasonable period in accordance with our agreement with the customer, subject to legal retention requirements.
  • Financial records: kept for at least 7 years as required by Australian tax and corporations law.
  • Logs and backups: kept on rolling windows for security and disaster recovery; deletions in production are propagated to backups over time.

8. Your rights under Australian privacy law

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you.
  • Correct information that is inaccurate, out of date, incomplete, or misleading.
  • Request that we delete your personal information, where we are not required to keep it.
  • Withdraw consent for marketing communications at any time.
  • Complain if you believe we have mishandled your information.

To exercise these rights, contact us at hello@day-zero.com.au. We will respond within a reasonable time and ordinarily within 30 days. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request.

If your information is held in one of our products because a customer organisation entered it, please contact that organisation directly. We will help them respond.

9. Cookies and similar technologies

Our website and products use cookies and similar technologies for:

  • Essential: keeping you signed in, remembering your session, and securing the service.
  • Functional: remembering preferences (for example, language or theme).
  • Analytics: understanding traffic patterns through Google Analytics on the website.

You can disable or delete cookies in your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies may prevent parts of our products from functioning.

10. Notifiable data breaches

If we become aware of an unauthorised access, disclosure, or loss of personal information that is likely to result in serious harm, we will notify affected individuals and the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) in accordance with the Notifiable Data Breaches scheme under Part IIIC of the Privacy Act.

11. Children's privacy

Our products are designed for use by businesses and their authorised staff. They are not directed at children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information directly from children under 16. If you believe we have inadvertently collected such information, please contact us so we can remove it.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision. If we make material changes, we will provide additional notice (for example, by email or in-product notification) where appropriate.

13. Contact us and complaints

For any privacy enquiry, request, or complaint:

  • Email: hello@day-zero.com.au
  • Postal: Day Zero Solutions Pty Ltd, Brisbane, Queensland, Australia (full registered office address available on request).

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at oaic.gov.au or on 1300 363 992.
